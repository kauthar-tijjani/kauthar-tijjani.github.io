Polymarket EDA: What Do Prediction Markets Actually Reveal?

A deep-dive exploratory analysis of Polymarket's open prediction market data. This project examines market structure, liquidity patterns, probability calibration, and trader behaviour across thousands of binary outcome markets — uncovering when crowds are wise and when they systematically drift.

  • Ingested and cleaned 52,400 market records; reduced null rate from 18% to 0.3%
  • Identified systematic overconfidence pattern 30 days before resolution
  • Built interactive Plotly calibration curves and category-level Brier scores
  • Political markets showed strongest calibration (Brier: 0.14); crypto worst (0.27)
  • Compared crowd consensus accuracy against individual analyst benchmarks

Calibration by Category
Political markets emerged as the most reliable predictors, with a mean Brier score of 0.14 — nearly twice as accurate as crypto markets (0.27). This likely reflects that political outcomes attract the most informed, research-driven participants. Crypto markets suffer from reflexivity: large traders can move the very probabilities they're betting on.

The dashboard revealed a strong Friday volatility effect in crypto markets — mean probability drift was 34% higher on Fridays compared to weekday averages. Regression Analysis

Liquidity & Accuracy Correlation Study

A statistical study examining whether higher trading volume and liquidity depth correlate with better final resolution accuracy across Polymarket categories. Spoiler: they do — but the relationship is non-linear and category-dependent.

  • Pearson r = −0.61 (volume vs. Brier score); Spearman ρ = −0.58
  • OLS regression with category fixed effects: R² = 0.44
  • Bootstrap CIs (n=5,000 samples) confirmed effect stability
  • Diminishing returns above $500K volume — accuracy plateaus
